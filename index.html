Secure Software Design — Case Study Profile

A fictitious campaign of digital protest targeting a multinational polluter.

Attacker Overview

motive • tactics • target • impact

Motive (why): Protest environmental harm by a global manufacturing firm; force public disclosure of practices and halt operations through reputational pressure.

Tactics (how): Website defacement, data "leak" mock, distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) bragging, social media banner/manifesto distribution.

Target (who/what): Public corporate website, investor-facing dashboards, and public relations channels of "ApexChem" .

Impact (so what): Temporary site outages, brand damage from leaked internal memo mock-ups, and amplified media scrutiny.

Timeline — Recon → Delivery/Initial Access → Actions/Impact

Recon (Weeks 1–2)
Public scraping of investor pages; mapping user emails; scanning for CMS misconfigurations (no exploit details shown).
Delivery / Initial Access (Week 3)
Phishing campaign to PR inboxes; social engineering leads to a low-privilege CMS user clicking a crafted link (mock scenario).
Actions / Impact (Week 4)
Defacement of homepage with manifesto, posting of mock internal memo, temporary DDoS-like high traffic (simulated). Public attention rose for 48 hours.

Quick Facts

Campaign name: SSD Attackers Case Study
Primary aim: Public accountability

Defender's Countermeasures

  1. Strict email filtering + DMARC/DKIM and phishing training for PR teams.
  2. Harden CMS accounts with MFA, least privilege, and strong patching.
  3. Public-facing rate limits & web application firewall to mitigate traffic spikes.
  4. Incident response playbook: rapid takedown, transparent comms, and forensic logging.
